My vision

During my first year at LCC I went throught multiple life-shaping experiences and grew as a personality. All of these internal changes only strenghten the image of a person I want to be, as well as the values I want to keep for a lifetime. Specifically, these are:

  • Self-growth
  • Integrity
  • Loyalty

As my personality stays on this three components, I'm constantly looking for ways to implement them from the mind to action. Therefore, in my freshman year, I decided to apply for a Residant Assistent position and to become a part of LDP. Learn more about how Leadership Development Program helped me in my journey!

My goals

Stepping into my leadership role, I understood that the most efficient way to reach a success as an RA is to set up goals that would correspond to my vision. Therefore, here are the objectives I established and continue following throught my leadership performance:

  • to stay consistent in words and actions
  • to be attentive to others
  • to have an equal attitude to everyone
  • to develop interpersonal relationships with residents
  • to be just in my decisions
  • to plan activities that would favor the community
  • to take up on challenges

After a semester of work on those goals, I can confidently say that they are working; furthermore, while implementing them I managed to establish a good-spirited atmosphere on the floor, where people feel safe and have opportunities to be engaged in community life.
